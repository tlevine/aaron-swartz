DFW: David Lynch Keeps His Head [in ASFTINDA]
By far, the most amazing article I have ever read. It's very long but never loses your interest; it combines an incredibly-detailed knowledge of cinematic history with a more thoughtful reflection and analysis than you'll see in any movie review with a classic DFW first-person description of the set. The whole thing is just so educational and thought-provoking and really uses its length to actually _work through_ issues instead of just glossing them that reading it feels like completing an entire college course.

Ezra Klein's blog
I rarely recommend whole blogs, but Ezra's become an addictive must-read. He's really grown into it this year and it's getting to the point where I'm almost unhealthily obsessed with him. Ezra's kind of me as I'd like to imagine myself: more informed, more influential, wittier, and a better writer. His blog tackles the intellectual aspects of DC: policy wonkery, strategic analysis, and food. My only complaint is that I wish he covered other topics with the same depth he uses for health care.

DFW's commencement speech
"Everybody worships. The only choice we get is what to worship. And pretty much anything you worship will eat you alive. If you worship money and things, you will never have enough. Worship your body and beauty and you will always feel ugly. Worship power, you will end up feeling weak and afraid, and you will need ever more power over others to numb you to your own fear. Worship your intellect, you will end up feeling stupid, a fraud, always on the verge of being found out. But the insidious thing about these forms of worship is not that they're evil or sinful, it's that they're unconscious. They are default settings."
